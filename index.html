Islamic History of Jerusalem

The Night Journey (Isra and Mi'raj)

Jerusalem is the site of Prophet Muhammad's (PBUH) miraculous Night Journey, where he traveled from Mecca to Al-Aqsa Mosque and ascended to the heavens. This event is mentioned in the Quran (Surah Al-Isra).

Al-Aqsa Mosque

Al-Aqsa Mosque is the third holiest site in Islam. It was the first Qibla (direction of prayer) for Muslims and remains a central place of worship and spiritual significance.

Islamic Rule in Jerusalem

Under Islamic rule, Jerusalem flourished as a center of culture, science, and religion. The city was conquered by Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab in 638 CE, and Muslims have maintained a strong presence ever since.

Ottoman Era

During the Ottoman Empire, Jerusalem saw significant development, including the restoration of its walls and the construction of many Islamic landmarks. The city remained a key religious and cultural hub.

About Jerusalem

The Old City of Jerusalem is a place of profound historical and spiritual significance, home to some of the most sacred sites in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Encircled by ancient walls, its narrow cobblestone streets lead to landmarks like the Western Wall, the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, and the Dome of the Rock. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Old City is a vibrant tapestry of cultures, traditions, and faiths, offering visitors a glimpse into thousands of years of history.
